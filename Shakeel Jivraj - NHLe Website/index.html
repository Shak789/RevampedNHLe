THE REVAMPED
NHL EQUIVALENCY MODEL

Welcome to the Revamped NHL Equivalency (NHLe) Model. Developed by Shakeel Jivraj, a statistics student at the University of Toronto, this model accurately computes how much one point from other leagues are worth the NHL. These conversion factors provide valuable insights into the offensive performance of forwards eligible for the 2023 NHL draft.

Section 2

Reimagining League Equivalencies
and Prospect Projections

Unlike previous versions of NHLe, this model focuses on paths
that players would most likely undergo when transitioning between
leagues to reflect the actual development of NHL prospects.
In addition, the model compares prosepcts of different ages and
projects their point totals.

Section 2

Bridging Continents: Conversion Factors
from Hockey Leagues Across the Globe

The model has computed the conversion factors from over 30 leagues worldwide.
It includes notable North American leagues, such as the AHL, CHL, USHL, and
NCAA, which are crucial in the development of NHL prospects. In addition,
the model considers top-tier European leagues, including the KHL, SHL, and
Liiga, along with their respective division 2 and minor leagues. Delve into
the league equivalencies and discover how prospects from different leagues
compare in terms of offensive production.
